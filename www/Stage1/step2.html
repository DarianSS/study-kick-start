Heart Stuff

Though we might sometimes wish we were able to escape our emotions, especially those pesky ones like sadness, fear, anxiety, shame and guilt living without them would be a very different place indeed. And not just in the positive sense! You may agree once you read on...

Yolanda feels anxious whenever she looks at her exam schedule - she's discovered that is she avoids looking at it she feels fine. However, she's now in the final week of revision and feeling desperate. She feels guilty for not starting earlier, anxious about how much work she now has to do, and angry with herself for getting into this situation.

Now, imagine for a moment that no-body feels emotions anymore. No more guilt, frustration, or anxiety. Would Yolanda start revising if she didn't have any emotions to get in her way?

Yes - she would be able to start her revision as the feelings wouldn't be distracting her anymore

No - she wouldn't be able to tell what is or isn't important in her life anymore.

So, we can't get rid of our feelings. Disappointed? Feeling worried that this means you've got to just put up with some unpleasant emotions that you just don't know what to do with them? Remember you'll be taught some techniques for managing the powerful experience of procrastination.

It is important to be able to name your emotions so that you know what you're dealing with. For many people it can be difficult to put a name to them. Don't worry if this is you! The words we use to name our emotions are just labels to try to help us understand what we're feeling. Whether you struggle with the labels or have a good grasp of them, the 'label game' will help you to boost your emotion familiarity.

A simple way to start building familiarity with emotions can be to think of all our emotions falling into four broad categories. On the next page you will see 16 words. Correctly place them onto one of the 4 hooks below within the time allowed and you'll earn a goodie for your bonus bag!
