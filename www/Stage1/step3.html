Body Stuff

Every time that Daniel thinks about preparing for his viva he goes all hot and sweaty, his heart pounds loudly and he hates the way his chest feels tight when he's breathing in and out. Daniel is experiencing his stress about his viva in a physical way.

We can often forget about our bodies especially when we are feeling well or are immersed in intellectual activity. For some people they only first notice their body when it starts to create unpleasant sensations for them. On an average day, Daniel will sit and listen to a lecture, debate in a seminar, or discuss some lab results in a peer group and his body just carries him around from A to B. He only notices his body when it starts to create these unpleasant sensations.

What's happening is that our bodies are experiencing a rush of energy. Our ancestors, like Cain the caveman, here, needed this energy to be able to react quickly to opportunities to capture food and to not be captured and become the food!

However, if you experience that same rush of energy when, like Daniel, you're only thinking about your viva, then you may well feel uncomfortable and maybe a bit puzzled about what's happening to you. It's probably not that helpful to run away like Cain did.

On seeing the Bear, Cain's fight or flight response was turned on. We can think of it as an alarm system. This is his body's way for gathering lots of energy to enable him to cope with threat by either fighting it or, as our Cain did, running away.

As a student at UCL you don't have to worry about bears or any other dangerous wildlife being at your door when you open it (maybe just the odd London rat). So when you get anxious about the work you need to do and the time you have left to do it, like Cain, your brain registers your alarm and this effects how your body responds.
