Do you remember Cain the Caveman? Life for him is very much lived in the present - he has to avoid getting eaten for long enough to catch himself something to eat. Even though you're not in danger of being eaten, your body still reacts in the same way as Cain's does.

Maybe this idea alarms you like it did for Daniel when he thought about preparing for his viva. His bodily sensations had him running for the door! In stage one you said that you experience (their choices). These bodily sensations can be with us for a long time and definitely make it harder to work. So what do you do?!

Fact: Studies suggest that brain activity prompted by exercise may play a particular role in improving memory and recall.

Now focus on what it feels like to have your bottom touching the chair. Focus on the weight that is resting on this part of your body. Notice the areas that take the most weight and those that don't. Make sure you are aware of what parts touch the chair and what parts don't. Continue this awareness and focus for 15 seconds.
